Product Design & Content Strategy

From Frustration to Flow at Nairobi National Museum

Streamlining the gateway to Kenya’s heritage through a frictionless, mobile-first digital ticketing experience.

Role

Lead Product & Content Designer

Focus

UX Audit, Content Strategy, Prototyping

The Problem: A Broken Gateway

A sentiment analysis of 2024–2026 visitor reviews revealed a systemic failure in the current ticketing flow. The primary barriers to entry were System Trust and Technological Friction caused by forced eCitizen account creation.

"First time in my life I can't get to a museum because I can't buy a ticket. The website was very slow and didn't accept my UK issued cards. After 20 mins, I just gave up."

— Oct 2025

"Extremely difficult process. You need to pay online via some government website. We are visiting so did not have WiFi or cell data."

— Feb 2024

"A convoluted, time-consuming system for getting around the absence of a Kenyan ecitizen or mobile account. I missed it."

— Aug 2024

The Content Strategy: 3 Pillars

1. Transparency

Clearly stating that the system is digital-only before the user even starts, setting accurate expectations immediately.

2. Validation

Ensuring visitors know exactly which ID categories apply to them to avoid "rejection at the gate" anxiety.

3. Alternative Paths

Providing a "Continue as Guest" option to bypass eCitizen login hurdles that frequently cause site crashes.

Step 01: The Hook

Setting the Stage

Old: "Buy Tickets"

New: "One ticket. Three worlds."

The museum isn't just one building; it's an experience (History, Wildlife, Culture). The "Three Worlds" copy justifies the price point and sets expectations for the scale of the visit.

Step 02: Selection

Bypassing the Gatekeeper

Primary Action: "Continue as guest"

Secondary Action: "Login with eCitizen"

By leading with "Continue as guest," we solve the #1 complaint on TripAdvisor: being forced to create a government account. We position eCitizen as an option for locals, not a barrier for tourists.

Step 03: Trust Engineering

Localized Payment Logic

"Both options are instant and secure."

This line specifically addresses the "fear of corruption" or "card theft" mentioned in reviews. Using familiar logos for Visa, Mastercard, and M-PESA visually reinforces security. For the M-PESA path, we number the steps to manage user anxiety during the 5-second waiting period.

Step 04: The Delivery

Designing for Dead Zones

Download as PDF

Reviewers complained about a lack of Wi-Fi at the gate. The "Download as PDF" button is a critical UX feature disguised as copy—it ensures the user has an offline-ready version before they arrive at the museum's dead zones.

Projected Outcomes

< 90s

Time-to-Ticket

Reduced from 45 minutes by removing the broken eCitizen mandatory flow.

+25%

Payment Success Rate

Offering M-PESA and Stripe/Card paths simultaneously without account requirements.

Zero

"I gave up" Reviews

Targeting elimination of abandonment by providing a clear, mobile-optimized guest checkout.
